📋 Reference Guide

Thresholds and definitions used throughout this dashboard.

Headcount Utilization Zones

Applies to the Total Utilization % shown in Overview, Growth Model, and KPI cards.
Utilization = Total Required Hours ÷ Total Available Hours per month.

Zone Utilization What it means
🟢 Healthy < 75% Comfortable buffer; team can absorb spikes and unplanned volume without strain.
🟡 Caution 75 – 85% Watch closely. Limited surge capacity. Begin evaluating headcount needs; start hiring process.
🔴 At Risk 85 – 90% Hiring trigger zone. Quality and burnout risk are real. Headcount decision should be in motion.
🚨 Over 90% — Hire Now > 90% Over the critical utilization threshold. A small hrs surplus may remain but there is no buffer for spikes, PTO, or volume growth. Headcount action is urgent.

Note: Model utilization tends to understate true load due to unplanned volume, training, and onboarding overhead. Treat the Caution zone (75%+) as the active hiring consideration trigger — by the time the model shows At Risk, you're already there operationally.

Order Capacity Flags

Applies to the Orders Capacity % shown in the Task Breakdown tab.
Order Capacity = Order Processing Hours Used ÷ Hours Available for Orders (after fixed ops).

Zone Threshold Action
🟢 OK < 80% Normal operations. No action needed.
🟡 Caution 80 – 90% Monitor daily volumes. Identify any tasks that can be deprioritized or automated.
🔴 At Risk > 90% Escalate. Team cannot sustain current order volume within available time.

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Effective FTE (Total OAs − 1) + Lead %. Accounts for lead time split between management and order work.
Net Hrs/OA Gross work hours minus lunch, breaks, and meeting time. Represents true productive hours per person per month.
Adj Orders Weighted daily orders × working days, scaled by the month's seasonal index.
Pharma Co Avg Weighted average number of pharma companies per order by segment (2.04–2.19). Each pharma co = 10 min of processing time.
Seasonal Index Monthly multiplier applied to order volume. Feb (1.22) and Mar (1.17) are peak months; Apr–Jun are softer (~0.86–0.89).
Growth Model Hrs Incremental hours from net-new accounts: onboarding setup time + recurring order processing for cumulative new accounts each month.
